$$ \Psi''(x) + \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2}\Psi(x) = 0 \\ \lambda = \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} $$ Can energy be negative? In this situation that would yield an exponential $\Psi$ function which cannot be zero at two points. For $E=0$ this is trivial, so we'll consider $E>0$

The characteristic polynomial is

$$ r^2 + \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} = 0 \\ r = \pm i\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} $$

This yields

$$ \Psi(x) = c_+ \cos(\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} x) + c_- \sin(\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} x)\\ \Psi(0) = 0 \implies 0 = c_{+} \cos(0) \implies c_+ = 0\\ \Psi(L) = 0 \implies 0 = c_- \sin(\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} L) $$

With $c\neq0$, we have $\sin(n\pi) = 0 $ this gives us $$ \frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} L = n \pi \\ \frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar} = \frac{n \pi}{L}\\ \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} = \left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2 $$

Thus all eigenvalues are $\left( \frac{n \pi}{L} \right)^2$, and the allowed energies are

$$ E = \frac{n^2 \pi^2\hbar^2}{2mL^2} $$

and the eigenfunctions are

$$ y_n(x) = \sin(\sqrt {\lambda_n} x) = \sin(\frac{n \pi}{L}x) $$

for $c_- = 1$ (eigenfunctions can be scaled)

Schrodigers equation can be split in the same way as $V(x)$

$$ \psi''(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{2m}{\hbar^2} \left(-V_0-E\right) \psi(x) & \text{for} & 0 \leq x\leq a\\ -\frac{2m}{\hbar^2}E \psi(x) & \text{for} & x\geq a \end{cases} $$ Remember that E<0 and can be represented correctly as E = -|E| $$ \psi''(x) = \begin{cases} -\frac{2m}{\hbar^2} \left(V_0-|E|\right) \psi(x) & \text{for} & 0 \leq x\leq a\\ \frac{2m}{\hbar^2}|E| \psi(x) & \text{for} & x\geq a \end{cases} $$

This gets us our k-values

$$ -k^2 = -\frac{2m}{\hbar^2} \left(V_0-|E|\right) \\ \kappa^2 = \frac{2m}{\hbar^2}|E| $$
